Useful Resources

The following materials offer background information, online documentation, and classroom-ready strategies that explain how teachers can migrate classes online in times of emergency.

Cohn, Jenae, & Seltzer, Beth. (2020). Teaching Effectively During Times of Disruption, for SIS and PWR. https://docs.google.com/document/d/1ccsudB2vwZ_GJYoKlFzGbtnmftGcXwCIwxzf-jkkoCU/preview#
This collection of explanations, documentation, and other resources from Stanford provides teacher-based details on how to move classes to online environment by using such tools as Canvas and Zoom. Includes details useful to those who teach writing. A PDF backup of the document (as of 03/10/2020) is also available.
Wernimont, Jacqueline, & Davidson, Cathy N. (2020). Teaching in the Context of COVID-19. https://docs.google.com/document/d/1yBE1cCqJ_4M-JZ62K4CefmYsZugqAWkGmZmdwESt0IM/preview
This collection of materials includes general information on prepping for online teaching in an emergency, resources to help teachers and students understand COVID-19, and resources for teaching online, including details on using HASTAC (Humanities, Arts, Science, and Technology Alliance and Collaboratory, called "haystack") resources. A PDF backup of the document (as of 03/10/2020) is also available.
